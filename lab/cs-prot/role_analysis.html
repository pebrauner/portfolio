How the system works ▼

Every CS2 team runs a resource hierarchy — a structured way of deciding which players receive money, utility, and favorable positioning each round. The rdy.gg model maps this hierarchy into Star Points, a per-player score that reflects how much the team's system invests in setting that player up to perform. The higher the Star Points, the more the system revolves around that player.

Role hierarchy — from highest to lowest priority
1 AWP The AWP specialist. Demands the highest priority budget because the AWP is the most resource-expensive weapon in the game. The AWPer always carries the highest Resources Δ on the team. ↑↑ Highest resources
2 ★ Star Rifler The system's primary rifle fragger. Gets the best rifle, the first utility, and the most favorable positions after the AWPer. Everything is built around getting this player into fights. ↑ High resources
3 2nd Star The second fragger — a player skilled enough to carry rounds when the primary star is neutralized. Reliable backup who can elevate into a star role situationally. → Mid resources
4 Support The utility engine of the team. Smokes, flashes, and grenades enable the stars' plays. Deliberately operates on fewer resources to fund the players above them. ↓ Low resources
5 IGL The in-game leader. Calls the strategy and sacrifices personal resource priority for the good of the system. The largest negative Resources Δ on the team almost always belongs here. ↓↓ Lowest resources
Metrics explained
Star Points Quantifies how much the team system invests in a player. Calculated separately for CT and T sides — a player can hold very different roles on each.
Res Δ Resources Delta — how much more (or less) money and utility a player receives versus the team average. Positive means the system funds them; negative means they fund others.
Util % Share of the team's total utility thrown by this player. High values signal a support-oriented role; low values indicate a player who relies on others to open space.
Aggression How actively a player contests space. Rated 0–5. High values mean frequent pushes and early duels; low values reflect a passive, reactionary playstyle.
Lurking How often a player separates from the team to apply pressure independently. Rated 0–5. IGLs and secondary fraggers often use lurking as a strategic tool.

rdy Score — Methodology Formula Placeholder

The rdy Score is a composite player performance index (0–100) that contextualizes raw stats within team system and role demands. Unlike traditional rating systems, it weights each contribution relative to the player's expected function — an IGL's utility impact is evaluated differently than an AWPer's. Scores are benchmarked against the top 50 active players globally.

Formula
rdy Score = w₁ × ADR_norm + w₂ × Impact_norm + w₃ × Role_adj + w₄ × Clutch_norm + w₅ × Utility_norm

Each component is normalized within the player's role cohort. Weights (w₁–w₅) are adjusted per role archetype. Final output is scaled 0–100. Specific coefficients will be finalized with the full model release.
